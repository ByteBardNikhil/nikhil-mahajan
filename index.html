About

I'm a Backend Engineer specializing in Java, Spring Boot, and AI-integrated systems. Currently at Bristlecone, I work at the intersection of enterprise integration engineering and AI — building automation frameworks powered by Google Gemini, managing connector version upgrades across cloud and on-premise business platforms on GCP, and resolving complex integration failures across large-scale enterprise systems.

One of my recent highlights: the team had been working for a month on a problem involving repeated cloud execution of SQL statements to analyze enterprise connector behavior. I joined, understood the problem, and within 2 days built a Python-based analysis utility in Google Colab that eliminated the need for cloud execution entirely — using Google Gemini to classify SQL operations locally. My manager recognized this publicly in front of the full team.

I also have hands-on experience evaluating LLM responses (ChatGPT, Gemini) from my time at Green Rider Technology, which gives me a unique perspective — I understand AI systems both as a builder and as an evaluator. This combination is what I bring to AI-integrated backend engineering.

I built Orchestrix, an event-driven distributed commerce platform using Java 21, Spring Boot microservices, Apache Kafka, Redis, PostgreSQL, gRPC, Docker, and React — deployed on GCP. It covers the full stack: authentication, inventory management, payment processing, and real-time notifications.

Outside work, I stay sharp on LeetCode (760+ problems, 320-day streak), play chess, and read books on self-growth. I'm actively looking to bring my backend and AI skills to a product-based company where I can build things at scale.
